Lisa Banks: Libraries

Volunteering

Atwater Library and Computer Centre

Circulation Desk

Since August 2024, I have been volunteering weekly at the Atwater Library's Circulation Desk. As the first person people encounter when entering the library, I'm also the person who sets the tone for their visit. By mainintaining a welcoming atmosphere, greeting every visitor with a smile, I hope to make their experience an enjoyable one. I also love chatting about what patrons are reading, and provide them with readers' advisory services in the process.

Digital Literacy Coach

In summer 2024, I volunteered weekly at the Atwater Library's Computer Centre as a support volunteer and digital literacy coach. In this position, I would direct users to resources related to their employment searches, troubleshoot technical issues, and offer one-on-one support to meet their needs.

Cataloging Volunteer, Centre Internationaliste Ryerson Fondation Aubin (CIRFA)

Throughout summer 2024, I was part of a team that completed original and copy cataloging of CIRFA's vast collection of materials, with a focus on their History of Socialism and the Labour Movement in Latin America section. Under the supervision of Christine Jacobs, metadata & cataloging librarian, I used modified RDA standards to catalog materials, which allowed for greater access and ease of use for patrons. I developed subject headings in both English and French, using Library of Congress Subject Headings and Répertoire de vedettes-matière de l'Université Laval, respectively. In this role, I developed experience working with materials in English, French, Spanish, and Brazilian Portuguese.

To see examples of my cataloging, click on the book covers on your right.
