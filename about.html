Lisa Banks: About

About Me

Hello, and thanks for stopping by! My name's Lisa, and I'm a librarian (fun fact: the primary database for library science research is Library & Information Science Abstracts—LISA! I was literally born for this career). Originally from Menaquesk (Saint John, New Brunswick), I've also lived in Fredericton, NB and am now based in Tiohtià:ke/Mooniyang (Montreal, QC). My academic background is in English literature, in which I hold a BA (Honours) and an MA. From 2015-2021, I pursued a doctorate in McGill's Department of English, before deciding to pursue a career in librarianship. In my work as a humanities scholar, I was interested in how modernist women think about illness, aging, and dying in their late works; I presented on just these topics at national and international conferences. I've also published academic articles and book reviews to help contribute to a vibrant academic community.

I bring this approach to my daily experience training as an information professional, and in the library itself. My approach is grounded in compassion, collaboration, intellectual honesty, and lifelong learning.

Why "stay curious"?

If you've spent time on this website, chances are you've noticed the tagline "stay curious." Library work is rooted in discovery and in access--in my professional practice and philosophy, each of these require curiosity. Curiosity has been a driver for my own work--whether as a humanities scholar untangling difficult modernist poetry, or now as a librarian helping a patron with whatever they bring to the desk. I hope that you, too, will embrace curiosity and stay curious.
